Killing Magic, We've All Done It

How to appreciate magic and not destroy it.

Have you ever caught a butterfly? Snatch it too fast, grasp it too tight, or keep it in a box too long; it will die.

“Examine each question in terms of what is ethically and esthetically right, as well as what is economically expedient. A thing is right when it tends to preserve the integrity, stability, and beauty of the biotic community. It is wrong when it tends otherwise.” Aldo Leopold, The Land Ethic, A Sand County Almanac.

Magic: (n.): an ellusive characteric of life, understood through faith; it is found everywhere and in everything.

As humans, we have tendencies to take things and manipulate environments. Though, we can only can only capture a simulation or representation of a thing. By altering something or its environment, its original essence is lost. Bearing witness and existing alongside magic is the only way we can experience something in its truest form.
